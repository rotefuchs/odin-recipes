Ham and Bananas Hollandaise

Behold, the 'Banana Ham-tastrophy'! A perplexing rendezvous of tropical sweetness and swine,
encapsulated in a dish that even Salvador Dali would find baffling. As though a banana and ham
had a dramatic falling out and ended up in therapy, only to be smothered by a questionable hollandaise
mediator. A culinary masterpiece for those who believe that food confusion builds character!



Ingredients

  • 6 medium bananas
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 6 thin slices boiled ham (about 1/2 lb)
  • 3 tablespoons prepared mustard
  • 2 envelopes (1 1/4-oz size) hollandaise sauce mix
  • 1/4 cup light cream

How to make
  1. Preheat oven to 400F. Lightly butter 2-quart, shallow baking dish.
  2. Peel bananas; sprinkle each with 1/2 tablespoon lemon juice, to prevent darkening.
  3. Spread ham slices with mustard. Wrap each banana in slice of ham.
    Arrange in single layer in casserole. Bake 10 minutes.
  4. Meanwhile, make sauce: In small saucepan, combine sauce mix with 1 cup water,
    1 tablespoon lemon juice, and cream. Heat, stirring, to boiling; pour over bananas.
    Bake 5 minutes longer, or until slightly golden. Nice with a green salad for brunch
    or lunch.

Makes 6 servings
